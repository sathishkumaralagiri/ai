# Choosing your AI tool

> ChatGPT vs Claude vs Microsoft Copilot — an honest comparison for Agile practitioners.

---

## The short answer

- **Use Claude** for nuanced facilitation language, coaching, and retrospective synthesis
- **Use ChatGPT** for structured outputs, long documents, and data-heavy tasks
- **Use Microsoft Copilot** if your organisation runs on M365 and you need Teams/SharePoint integration

All three can handle the core Scrum Master use cases. The differences are at the margins — but they matter for specific tasks.

---

## Privacy — what you need to know

**Consumer tiers (free/standard plans):** Your inputs may be used to train models unless you opt out. Never paste real names, personal information, or confidential business data.

**Enterprise/Team tiers:** Data is not used for training. Check your organisation's policy before using any AI tool with sensitive content.

**Practical rule:** Use fictional team names and anonymised data in consumer tools. Use real data only on enterprise-licensed tools with data processing agreements in place.

---

## Starting recommendation

If you're just getting started: **use Claude** (claude.ai — free tier). It handles agile facilitation language better than any other tool, and the free tier is generous enough for daily Scrum Master use.

Once you're comfortable: add ChatGPT for structured documents, and Microsoft Copilot if your organisation adopts it.
