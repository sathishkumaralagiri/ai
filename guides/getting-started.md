# Getting started with AI as a Scrum Master

> Your practical first week — no hype, no overwhelm.

---

## Why this matters

AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Microsoft Copilot can save a Scrum Master 3–5 hours per sprint on ceremony admin — freeing you to focus on coaching, impediment removal, and team health. But most practitioners either don't start at all, or start badly and give up.

This guide gets you to a useful output in your first session.

---

## Week 1 — three things to try

### Day 1: Draft your next Sprint Goal

Take your planned sprint backlog and paste it into Claude or ChatGPT with this prompt:

```
You are an experienced Scrum Master. Based on the following planned stories,
draft 3 versions of a Sprint Goal that are outcome-focused, not task-focused.

Stories: [paste your stories here]
Business context: [one sentence about what the team is working towards]
```

You'll get a draft in seconds. Edit it with your team — the AI does the first draft, the team owns the final version.

### Day 3: Synthesise a retrospective

After your next retro, paste all the sticky notes into Claude:

```
You are an Agile Coach. Below are the raw inputs from our team retrospective.
Identify the top 3 themes, do a 5-Whys on the biggest one,
and suggest 2 SMART action items.

[paste sticky notes]
```

### Day 5: Write a stakeholder update

```
You are a Scrum Master writing a post-sprint update for senior leadership.
Sprint goal: [goal]
Completed: [X] of [Y] stories ([Z] points)
Key deliveries: [list]
Status: Green / Amber / Red — [one-line reason]

Write a 100-word update in a confident, factual tone.
```

---

## Common mistakes to avoid

| Mistake | Why it hurts | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Vague prompts | Generic output that isn't useful | Add role, context, and format instructions |
| No editing | You sound like a robot | Always review and personalise |
| Sharing raw AI output | Erodes trust | Edit before you send |
| Pasting personal data | Privacy risk | Use anonymised or fictional names |
| Expecting perfection first time | Leads to giving up | Iterate — treat the first output as a draft |

---

## The mindset shift

Think of AI as a **first-draft machine and a thinking partner**, not an oracle. Your job is to give it good context, review what it produces, and add your coaching judgement on top.

The best Scrum Masters using AI aren't the ones who trust it most — they're the ones who know when to push back on it.
